Data is what is missing to Bacon's spider, she is spinning an algorithm on lacking data. The ant has plenty of data, but does not make anything of it. The ant is a martyr to society, the spider is a solitary predator. It has two legs more. Not many spiders can live in the same ecosystem: they would be lacking prey. For bees I do not know what to make of the metaphor, as there are forager bees that bring the pollen, and hive bees who digest the honey and heat it to decrease its water concentration.

The danger of ideas and words is that they have no relief. Relief is perceived in the experience of reality. But before that, any idea is as good as another.

They say the butcher sublimes a killing will in his work. What does the creator sublimes? A demiurgic arrogance?

'Errare humanum est', errors are human, but more than that, isn't Everything we do an error? 'Errors are almost always sacred' says Dali.

Can we keep in our mind at the same time our good and evil dimension? Can we continually perceive our duality? Being able to not be under the illusion that we are completely good would bring us closer to a truthful behaviour.

I see a high proximity between the philosopher and the artist. Both are thinkers, but they are concerned with truth at different stages. I would say the artist is anterior to the philosopher, but do correct me if I am wrong.

The song Them Heavy People by Kate Bush is a splendid description of the social redemption/return of the secluded artist. The other, a threat, a blessing!

Ivan Illitch chose a wife that was both a joy to him and to society (at least in the beginning).

The true bottleneck and vacillation of life is the will to live, which seems to have as non-unique condition the "other" as the witness of the dignity of our story. In this regard, "a community of one's own" could be seen as a necessity for mental stability.
As Camus said, the only question that exists is the one of suicide. I agree and add: We can be dead spiritually even though we are physically alive. This can happen wittingly or unwittingly. To be a living-dead is to refuse to live and to refuse suicide, that is to be in limbo. That state solidifies with time, but has liquidity.

For book search, physical tasting of a limited curated list of random options seems vastly superior to any sort of conceptual approach. Randomness is suitable as it makes it possible to find what we were not looking for. Ordering references is soulless. Meeting with an object is deliciously contingent.

The hiking experience is useful as a metaphor of work.

The Greeks valued the power of the spoken word, and it was their main method of communication and storytelling. Bahn and Bahn write, "To Greeks the spoken word was a living thing and infinitely preferable to the dead symbols of a written language." Socrates himself believed that once something has been written down, it lost its ability for change and growth. For these reasons, among many others, oral storytelling flourished in Greece. To live towards death, or to live towards the end of such event in our life. I live toward the end of that run, or toward the end of that party.
