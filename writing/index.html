Ivan Illitch chose a wife that was both a joy to him and to society (at least in the beginning).

The true bottleneck and vacillation of life is the will to live, which seems to be brought by the "other" as the witness of the dignity of our story. As Camus said, the only question that exists is the one of suicide. I agree and add: We can be dead spiritually even though we are physically alive. This can happen wittingly or unwittingly. To be a living-dead is to refuse to live and to refuse suicide, that is to be in limbo. That state solidifies with time, but has liquidity.

For book search, physical tasting of a limited curated list of random options seems vastly superior to any sort of conceptual approach. Randomness is suitable as it makes it possible to find what we were not looking for.

The hiking experience is useful as a metaphor of work.
